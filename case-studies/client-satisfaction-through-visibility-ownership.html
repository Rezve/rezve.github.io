Bridging the Gap

1. Understanding Client Priorities

I sat with the product owner and asked:

  • What are your highest-priority items?
  • What features do you absolutely need?
  • What critical bugs must be fixed immediately?

From this, I created a clear, structured backlog.

2. Collaborating with the Team

Next, I worked with the team to estimate tasks‚Äînot dictating, but guiding. I played the role of the product owner at times, asking:

  • Why does this task take this long?
  • What dependencies are involved?
  • Can we validate this from the code or database level?

Once the team was confident, we presented our plan to the product owner. We committed to delivering specific high-priority items in the next two weeks, ensuring alignment.

3. Introducing Process Discipline

I formalized a sprint process to eliminate chaos:

  • Product owner defines priorities at the start of the sprint.
  • Team commits to a two-week delivery cycle.
  • No mid-sprint changes unless it's an emergency.
  • Urgent issues are handled with structured adjustments, not ad-hoc interruptions.

After the meeting, I posted our plan and targets in the shared group, ensuring full transparency.
